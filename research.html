Research


It is never too early to get started in research!

As a science major, it pays to have hands-on experience in your field. This is especially true for those who are planning to continue their education in graduate programs or intend to work in the field! Even the B.S. in Neuroscience degree requires at minimmum 3 credit hours spent in a laboratory, demonstrating the importance of practical experience.

Tips from graduating seniors:


  • Get involved early! It takes time to learn lab skills and earn the trust of your teammates. You could also have the opportunity to work in the same lab for a 2 or 3 more years, which might even afford you the opportunity to end up as a co-author on a publication, or earn your own independent project.
  • Find a faculty member and ask them to be your mentor. Having a mentor as a student can be an invaluable experience in directing your growth and connecting you to valuable resources.
  • Talk to your professors and let them know you are interested in joining a lab! They may be able to steer you in the direction of their own lab or even to another colleague.
  • Not every lab has availability or space for an undergraduate assistant. Do not feel discouraged if you are not accepted. It takes time to find the right match!
